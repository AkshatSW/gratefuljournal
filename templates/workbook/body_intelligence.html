What Is Body Intelligence?

Body intelligence is about how aware you are of your body, what you know about your body and what you actually do for and with your body. The body is an intelligent organism capable of acting independently of the mind via its self-regulation and feedback systems. Each cell in our body holds memory and forms neural pathways which determine our response depending on our previous experiences. The body is the only place through which the mind can function. Mind and Body are interlinked with each other. Hence it becomes even more important to be aware of your body & build a healthy & happy relationship with the body

    Tips to apply in your day to day life

  1. 1. Meditate using your breath and body movements
  2. 2. Spend at least a minute every day being mindful with your body
  3. 3. Dancing, and exercising (doing these activities mindfully will help you get to know your body and its capacity better)

    Affirmations:

  1. 1.I appreciate my body for everything it does for me
  2. 2. I am perfect, whole, and complete just the way I am
  3. 3. I feed my body healthy nourishing food and give it healthy nourishing exercise because it deserves to be taken care of

The Connection Between Body and Emotions

Awareness :

Our mind and body are connected. Emotions are felt in our bodies. For example, when you are stressed, you may feel your neck or head aching. That’s your body’s way of responding to stress. You can use your body to release this stress by moving those body parts consciously.

Tips to apply in your day-to-day life:

  1. 1. Becoming aware of the sensations in your body will help you understand your emotions better
  2. 2. Naming the emotions that you feel rather than saying “I am fine” or “I am good”. This will help you feel more in touch with yourself and will make you feel more understood and confident in yourself

Affirmations:

  1. 1. My body is a safe haven for all my emotions
  2. 2. I trust my body to tell me everything it needs in perfect time
  3. 3. My mind is brilliant; my body is healthy; my soul is tranquil

Understanding 5 Elements. Their relevance with our body and day-to-day Life

The universe is made up of the five elements: Air, Water, Fire, Earth, and Sky. Understanding these elements helps us better understand both the physical laws of nature and the constitution of our bodies.
Earth and water are positioned at the bottom of the body, below the navel; fire is set in the centre of the torso, and air and space are found in the upper body. This is the natural order of the elements in the body.
The elements have been understood by humans in their physical aspects. Humans have a close relationship with nature, and our surroundings have a significant impact on both our physical and mental health. When closely observed, the elements of nature reveal the wisdom necessary for a healthy lifestyle.

Air: The element of air signifies purity and the ability to keep moving upward. Highs and lows are a part of life, but rising after every fall is what makes us stronger. It doesn't matter how many times you fall in life; how well you get back up and go forward truly matters.
Water: The element of water represents the flow. Just as the water, the free-flowing of energy, and our emotions, without any judgment or resistance are what the natural flow is all about.
Fire: The element of fire is about passion and confidence. Anything that comes into contact with fire will burn, but we can control what will burn. We need to burn with this fire all unfavourable thoughts that come to mind and have an impact on us.
Earth: The element of earth represents grounding. It provides strength, stability, balance, calmness, grounding, and resilience
Space: The space element is vast, unlimited, free, and boundless; it has no ego and contains everything of creation. The problem arises when we incorrectly use the limitless capacity. Being selfless in daily life is the first correct utilization of this infinite capacity. Your mind grows more open, unrestricted, and silent the more open and accepting you are.

Connection with Emotions and Body Pain

Awareness

For our body, pain, whether emotional or physical, is linked to changes in the prefrontal cortex and cingulate cortex. Therefore, there is no particular way for our brain to figure out whether this is physical pain or emotional pain and therefore they both feel almost the same way for human beings. When our body perceives this emotional pain, it starts responding to this impact like any physical pain.

Tips to apply in your day-to-day life:

  1. 1. To be aware that EVERYTHING our body does is its way of protecting us from the perceived threat
  2. 2. Develop a healthier relationship with your body. Start communicating with your body compassionately.

Affirmations:

  1. 1. My body is my protector.
  2. 2. I trust my body to tell me everything it needs in perfect time.
  3. 3. It is safe for me to connect with my body.
